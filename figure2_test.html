(A) Contact matrices show that loop domains in untreated HCT-116-RAD21-mAC cells (top) disappear after auxin treatment (bottom). Three representative loci are shown (at 10kb resolution): chr8:133.8-134.6Mb (left), chr4:40.8-42.1Mb (middle) and chr1:91.9-95.8Mb (right).

(B) Aggregate peak analysis (APA (Rao et al., 2014)) was used to measure the aggregate strength of the links associated with all loop domains in low-resolution Hi-C contact maps generated across a time course of auxin treatment and withdrawal. APA scores are shown on top; values greater than 1 indicate the presence of loops. APA plots for each time point are shown on the bottom; the strength of looping is indicated by the extent of focal enrichment at the center of the plot. Loop domains are rapidly lost as cohesin is degraded, and quickly restored when auxin is withdrawn (See Fig S2B).

(C) The blue lines represent individual loop reformation curves for each of 1,988 loop domains. The plots are scaled so that the number of contacts in the untreated map corresponds to a value of 1, and the number of contacts in the auxin-treated map corresponds to 0. Median curves for all loop domains (black), the top 5% of loop domains (in terms of speed of recovery, see Methods; green), and the bottom 5% of loop domains (red) are shown. Error bars indicate 25th and 75th percentile for each set of loops.

(D) Enrichment of epigenetic features within a loop domain as a function of the loop domainâ€™s speed of recovery (see Methods), showing the slowest 5%, the 30th-35th percentile, the 65th-70th percentile, and the fastest 5%. Enrichment is with respect to the average value of the feature across all the intervals spanned by loop domains (see Methods). Superenhancers and strong NIPBL peaks are particularly enriched in fast loop domains vs. slow loop domains. Repressive histone modifications exhibit the opposite trend.

(E) Two examples of regions containing fast loop domains (1st row: chr18:67.6-68.4Mb; 2nd row: chr14:68.2-69.5Mb) and two examples of regions containing slow loop domains (3rd row: chr5:95.5-96.15Mb; 4th row: chr12:91.15-91.95Mb) are shown, along with ChIP-Seq tracks (from auxin-treated cells) for NIPBL, H3K4me1, H3K4me3, and H3K27Ac. For fast loop domains, reformation is apparent by 20-40 minutes after auxin withdrawal, whereas for slow loop domains, reformation is not seen until 3 hours after auxin withdrawal.
